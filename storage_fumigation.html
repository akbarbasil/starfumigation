Storage Fumigation

Storage fumigation is the process of using fumigants (chemical gases or vapors) to eliminate pests from stored goods, warehouses, or storage facilities. This method is commonly used in industries where products are stored for long periods, such as food storage, grain silos, warehouses, and even in logistics for containerized goods. The aim is to eradicate pests that could infest the stored items, ensuring the goods remain in good condition and free of contamination.

01. Key Aspects of Storage Fumigation

Storage areas are highly susceptible to pest infestations over time. Standard sanitation often isn't enough to reach deep-seated pests. Fumigation delivers comprehensive protection:

Complete Pest Control

Eliminates active insects, larvae, rodents, and mites that infest food products, grains, raw commodities, and fabrics in storage facilities.

Prevent Material Damage

Pests like weevils, beetles, or moths feed on stored agricultural cargo, causing extreme product degradation and financial losses.

Hygiene & Safety

Sanitizes storage zones and prevents the accumulation of toxic pest droppings, mold, and secondary fungal growth in humid warehouse areas.

02. Types of Pests Treated

Our chemical gases penetrate deep within stacked cargo and complex building structures to eliminate diverse pests:

Stored Product Insects

Grain weevils, rice moths, flour beetles, and larvae feeding inside grains and dry food products.

Rodents & Vermin

Rats and mice nesting in warehouses that contaminate bulk goods and chew packaging.

Mites, Fungi & Mold

Treatment to control mildew growth and micro-pests in damp or poorly ventilated silos.

Textile & Boring Pests

Carpet beetles, fabric moths, and termites targeting stored clothes, raw cotton, or wood items.

03. Fumigants Used in Storage Fumigation

We select specialized, approved gas compounds tailored to the type of stored commodity:

Phosphine Gas PH₃

Extremely popular for raw agricultural grains, dry cocoa, seeds, and packaged foods. Leaves zero residues when ventilated.

Sulfuryl Fluoride SF

Commonly applied to empty warehousing structures, raw timber, and commercial items containing active beetle colonies.

Methyl Bromide CH₃Br

Used under strict quarantine guidelines for express treatments of wooden packaging and import/export warehouses.

Carbon Dioxide CO₂

Utilized for controlled atmosphere treatments, particularly in organic food storage and organic grain silos.

04. The Storage Fumigation Process

  • Pre-Treatment Inspection: We assess the warehouse size, active pest types, humidity levels, and nearby residential zones before preparing a work plan.
  • Hermetic Sealing: Crucial step. We seal gaps, vents, and doors using heavy-duty industrial gas-barrier sheets to lock the fumigants inside.
  • Gas Introduction: Injecting fumigant at calculated dosage ratios based on volume, temperature, and specific target pests.
  • Exposure Window: The area is kept isolated for 24 to 72 hours, maintaining gas concentration levels to eradicate all egg and pupae stages.
  • Degassing & Safe Entry: Post-treatment ventilation clears residual gases. We inspect with specialized electronic monitors before issuing a safe-entry certificate.

05. Benefits of Storage Fumigation

  • Deep Penetration: Gas flows into deep grain stacks, bags, and hard-to-reach ceiling crevices where standard liquid sprays cannot reach.
  • Preventive Quarantine: Treating raw goods prior to warehousing prevents cross-contamination of other clean sections.
  • Zero Solid Residue: Approved gases evaporate completely during ventilation, ensuring the treated grain remains edible and free of chemical paste residues.

06. Safety Considerations & Evacuation

Vital Safety Protocols

Supervision: Storage fumigation is high-risk and must only be carried out under the supervision of government-licensed pest quarantine experts.

Zone Evacuation: The warehouse and adjoining buffer zones must be completely evacuated during the exposure cycle. Clear danger warning signs are posted.

Regulatory Clearance: Star Fumigation provides all official paperwork, including treatment logs and official compliance certifications required by environmental laws.

07. When is Storage Fumigation Needed?

  • Active Warehouse Infestations: If visual pests or high temperature spots are detected in bulk grain silos or textile stacks.
  • Export Commodity Preparation: Before agricultural raw materials are packaged and dispatched overseas.
  • Annual Preventive Cycles: Pre-monsoon or pre-harvest warehouse sanitization to welcome new food stocks safely.
